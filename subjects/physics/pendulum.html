🕰️ Simple Pendulum Lab

Investigate T = 2π√(L/g). Adjust length, record period, plot T² vs L to determine g. Standard WAEC/NECO physics practical.

📊 Recorded Data

#L(px)T(s)T²(s²)

📖 Theory: The period T of a simple pendulum depends only on its length L and gravity g. The relationship T = 2π√(L/g) can be verified by plotting a graph of T² against L. The slope of this graph equals 4π²/g, from which the value of g can be calculated.

🎯 WAEC Practical: This is a standard WAEC/NECO physics practical question. Students are required to measure the period for different pendulum lengths, record their readings in a table, plot T² against L, determine the slope, and calculate the acceleration due to gravity.
